📖 Sterilization Education

🔬

How Autoclaves Work

Autoclaves kill microorganisms using pressurized steam at high temperatures. The combination of heat, pressure, and time destroys all bacteria, viruses, fungi, and spores, including hard-to-kill organisms like Geobacillus stearothermophilus.

⚗️

Why Spore Tests Matter

Biological indicators (spore tests) use highly resistant bacterial spores to verify your autoclave is actually killing microorganisms. Mechanical indicators (temperature/pressure gauges) can fail while appearing normal. Spore tests are the only way to confirm true sterilization.

⚠️

Common Mistakes

Overloading the chamber, using incorrect wrapping, not allowing proper drying time, and skipping spore tests are common errors. These can result in incomplete sterilization, putting clients at risk of infection and studios at risk of legal liability.

📋

Documentation Requirements

Most states require documented proof of sterilization compliance. This includes daily cycle logs, spore test results, maintenance records, and staff training documentation. Keep records for at least 3 years for health inspections and potential legal protection.

🚨

When Spore Tests Fail

STOP using the autoclave immediately if a spore test fails. Recall all instruments processed since the last successful test. Do NOT use instruments until the problem is identified, fixed, and a new spore test passes. Contact clients if needed.

🔄

Regular Maintenance

Weekly inspection of gaskets and chamber, monthly deep cleaning, annual professional calibration, and immediate repair of any issues are essential. Preventive maintenance prevents costly breakdowns and ensures consistent sterilization performance.

⚠️ Critical Safety Notice

This calculator provides general guidance based on CDC, FDA, and manufacturer recommendations. Always follow your autoclave manufacturer's specific instructions and your state/local health regulations. This tool does not replace professional training or regulatory compliance requirements.

Sterilization is a critical safety process. Improper sterilization can result in serious infections and legal liability. When in doubt, consult with a sterilization specialist, your autoclave manufacturer, or your local health department.

State and local regulations vary. While we strive to maintain accurate regulatory information, requirements can change. Always verify current requirements with your local health department.

Failed spore test = STOP using autoclave immediately until issue is resolved and subsequent test passes. Using instruments from a failed cycle can endanger clients.
